# madprojx — Brand Positioning

## One-Liner

AI consulting for small businesses — implemented securely, explained clearly.

## Who We Serve

Small business owners who know they need AI but don't know where to start. They're hearing about it from every direction — vendors, competitors, employees, the news — and they're overwhelmed. They don't have a technical team. They don't have an AI strategy. They're worried about making an expensive mistake, exposing customer data, or picking the wrong tool and getting locked in.

These are not tech companies. They're service businesses, retail operations, local firms, and trades — 5 to 100 employees — trying to stay competitive without getting burned.

## What We Do

We bridge the gap between "we should use AI" and "here's exactly how, what it costs, and what it does for your business." We assess, recommend, implement, and advise — with security baked into every step, not bolted on after.

We don't sell AI products. We don't take commissions from vendors. We work for the client, not the tool.

## Why madprojx

**Kevin brings 20+ years in cybersecurity (CISSP) to AI consulting.** Most AI consultants can implement. Very few can implement safely. Small businesses are the most vulnerable to data leaks, misconfigured AI tools, shadow AI usage, and vendor lock-in — and the least equipped to evaluate those risks.

madprojx doesn't just help businesses adopt AI. We help them adopt AI without creating new problems.

## What We Don't Do

- We don't build custom AI models or train LLMs
- We don't sell AI software or take vendor commissions
- We don't do enterprise-scale deployments (50+ seat rollouts)
- We don't promise AI will replace employees — we show where it amplifies them

## Brand Voice

**Tone:** Direct, practical, security-aware. No hype. No jargon unless the audience is technical. Explain things the way you'd explain them to a smart business owner who doesn't live in tech.

**Vocabulary:**
- Use "implement" not "deploy"
- Use "protect" not "harden"
- Use "evaluate" not "audit" (audit sounds expensive and scary)
- Use "your data" not "data assets"
- Say what AI actually does in plain terms — "reads your invoices and flags errors" not "leverages NLP for document intelligence"

**Do:**
- Lead with the business outcome, not the technology
- Acknowledge that AI skepticism is reasonable
- Be honest about what AI can't do
- Use real numbers and timeframes when possible

**Don't:**
- Promise ROI you can't prove
- Use "revolutionary," "game-changing," or "cutting-edge"
- Talk about AI capabilities without connecting them to a business problem
- Assume the reader knows what an LLM, API, or prompt is

## Differentiators

1. **Security-first AI** — Every recommendation includes a risk assessment. Not as an add-on. As the foundation.
2. **Plain language** — We explain AI in terms business owners actually understand.
3. **Vendor-neutral** — We recommend the right tool, not the one that pays us.
4. **Solo practitioner** — You work directly with Kevin. No handoffs, no junior staff, no account managers.
5. **Small business focus** — We understand the constraints: limited budget, limited time, limited technical staff. Our recommendations fit that reality.
