# madprojx — Content Strategy

## Purpose

Build trust, demonstrate expertise, and create inbound demand from small business owners considering AI. Every piece of content should make the reader think: "This person understands my situation and I trust them to help."

## Platform Priority (in launch order)

### Tier 1 — Launch First (Week 3-4)

**LinkedIn**
- Primary platform. This is where small business owners and decision-makers are.
- Cadence: Daily (5-7 posts per week)
- Format mix:
  - Educational posts (60%) — "5 things to check before giving an AI tool access to your customer data"
  - Personal perspective posts (25%) — "I've been in cybersecurity for 20 years — here's what scares me about how small businesses are adopting AI"
  - Case study / example posts (15%) — Anonymized or hypothetical scenarios showing before/after AI implementation
- Length: 150-300 words per post. Punchy. One idea per post.
- Always end with a question or CTA that invites engagement

**Blog (madprojx.com)**
- Cadence: 1 long-form article per week
- Format: 800-1,500 words. SEO-targeted.
- Topics should answer questions small business owners are actually searching:
  - "How can a small business use AI safely?"
  - "What AI tools are worth paying for in 2026?"
  - "Is my customer data safe with AI tools?"
  - "How to evaluate AI vendors as a small business"
- Each blog article becomes source material for 2-3 LinkedIn posts and multiple X posts

### Tier 2 — Add After Tier 1 Is Running (Week 5-6)

**X (Twitter)**
- Cadence: 3-5 posts per week (repurposed from LinkedIn and blog)
- Format: Shorter, punchier takes. Threads for deeper topics.
- Use SuperGrok to find active conversations about small business + AI and engage authentically
- Don't just broadcast — reply, quote, and add value to existing discussions

### Tier 3 — Add When Tier 1 and 2 Are Consistent (Phase 3+)

**Instagram**
- Visual tips, infographics, short reels about AI security basics
- Lower priority but good for credibility and reach outside LinkedIn's demographic
- Repurpose blog content into carousel posts

**YouTube**
- Animated explainer videos under 5 minutes
- Topics: "What is AI doing with your business data?" / "3 AI tools every small business should know about"
- Simple production — screen recordings, animated slides, voiceover
- This is the longest-term play. Don't start until LinkedIn and blog are shipping consistently.

## Strategic Shift: Daily Content + Passive Income Layer

As of March 2026, madprojx content strategy expands from consulting-support content to a full thought leadership engine. The goal is building an audience that generates passive income streams alongside consulting revenue.

**What changed:**
- LinkedIn cadence increases from 2-3x/week to daily (5-7 posts/week)
- Content production model shifts to voice-memo-driven pipeline (manual now, automated on KITT by month 2-3)
- Platform expansion is phased — LinkedIn + blog first, then X + newsletter, then video, then paid products
- Every piece of content serves dual purpose: builds audience AND supports consulting credibility
- Monetization channels unlock over time: affiliate → digital products → sponsorships → courses

**What didn't change:**
- Brand voice, pillars, and positioning remain identical
- Security angle in 1 of every 3 pieces minimum
- Claude drafts, Kevin publishes — no auto-posting ever
- Quality bar stays the same — consistency beats volume if volume means garbage

## Content Pillars

All content maps to one of these four themes:

1. **AI Security for Small Business** — Your differentiator. Data privacy, vendor risk, shadow AI, access controls. This is what separates madprojx from every other AI consultant.

2. **AI Made Simple** — Demystifying AI for non-technical business owners. What it actually does, what it costs, where it fits, where it doesn't.

3. **Practical AI Use Cases** — Specific, real examples of AI helping small businesses. Invoice processing, customer service, scheduling, reporting. Concrete, not theoretical.

4. **AI Mistakes to Avoid** — What goes wrong when businesses adopt AI without guidance. Fear-based content done responsibly — not to scare, but to educate.

## Weekly Workflow

1. **Daily:** Capture voice memos, observations, and ideas. Draft or finalize one LinkedIn post. Engage on LinkedIn (15-20 min — comment on others' posts, respond to comments on yours).
2. **Monday:** Review weekly content brief (generated by Cowork scheduled task). Map topics to daily pillar framework for the week.
3. **Wednesday:** Draft blog article in Claude Cowork. Store drafts in `areas/content/drafts/`.
4. **Thursday:** Edit, finalize, and publish blog article.
5. **Friday:** Engage on LinkedIn and X. Find new conversations via SuperGrok. Review weekly metrics.

## Content Rules

- Never publish AI-generated content without editing it. Claude drafts, Kevin publishes.
- Every post must connect to a business outcome, not just a technology feature.
- No jargon unless you define it in the same sentence.
- If you can't explain the value in one sentence, the topic isn't ready.
- Security angle should appear in at least 1 out of every 3 posts — it's your differentiator, keep it visible.
- Consistency beats quality. A good post published beats a perfect post stuck in drafts.

## Metrics to Track (Keep It Simple)

- LinkedIn: impressions, engagement rate, profile views, connection requests per week
- Blog: page views, time on page, top-performing articles
- X: impressions, replies, follower growth
- Overall: inbound inquiries (DMs, emails, contact form submissions) — this is the only metric that actually matters for revenue
