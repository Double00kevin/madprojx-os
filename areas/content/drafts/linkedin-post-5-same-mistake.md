# LinkedIn Post 5 — "Three businesses, same mistake"

**Pillar:** AI Mistakes to Avoid
**Status:** Draft
**Target publish:** 2026-03-19

---

Three business owners called me this month.

Different industries. Different sizes. Same mistake.

They all signed up for an AI tool, uploaded real customer data during the free trial, and never checked what the tool does with that data.

Two of them were using tools that explicitly say they use uploaded data to train their models. It's right there in the terms — paragraph 4, section B, in language designed to be skipped.

The third was using a tool with a "business" tier that doesn't train on your data. But he was on the free tier. Different rules.

Here's the thing — none of these people were careless. They're sharp business owners. They just assumed AI tools work like other software.

They don't.

With most software, your data is stored and used to run the service. With many AI tools, your data is stored, used to run the service, AND used to improve the product for everyone else.

That's a fundamentally different deal. And most small businesses don't realize they agreed to it.

Before you upload anything to any AI tool, find the answer to one question:

"Is my data used to train your models?"

If you can't find the answer in 60 seconds, that's your answer.

What AI tools is your business using right now? Have you checked?
