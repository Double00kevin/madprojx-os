# LinkedIn Post 2 — "Stop asking should we use AI"

Stop asking "should we use AI?"
Start asking "where are we wasting 10 hours a week?"

That's the question that actually leads somewhere. Because AI doesn't solve vague problems. It solves specific, repetitive ones.

A 12-person accounting firm I know was spending 8 hours a week manually pulling data from client documents and entering it into their system. Every week. Same process, different documents.

They set up one AI tool — $50/month — that reads the documents and extracts the data automatically. Their team reviews the output instead of doing the entry.

8 hours became 45 minutes.

They didn't need an AI strategy. They didn't need a consultant to tell them AI is important. They needed someone to point at the bottleneck and say "that one — AI can fix that."

If you're a small business owner wondering where to start with AI: don't start with the technology. Start with the time. Find the task your team hates doing, the one that eats hours every week and never changes.

That's your first AI project.

Where does your team lose the most time?
