# LinkedIn Post 3 — "Read the terms"

**Pillar:** AI Security for Small Business
**Status:** Draft
**Target publish:** TBD

---

I've been in cybersecurity for 20 years. I've seen businesses get burned by every kind of technology adoption gone wrong.

But I've never seen anything move as fast as AI — or as recklessly.

Here's what I'm watching happen with small businesses right now:

They sign up for an AI tool because it has a great demo. They upload real customer data during the free trial. They don't read the terms. The tool uses their data to train its models. They can't undo it.

This isn't theoretical. It's happening every day. And small businesses are the most vulnerable because they don't have a legal team reviewing vendor contracts or an IT department evaluating data policies.

The three questions I tell every business owner to ask before giving any AI tool access to their data:

1. Where does my data go after I upload it?
2. Is my data used to train your models?
3. What happens to my data if I cancel?

If the vendor can't answer these in one paragraph, walk away.

AI can absolutely help your business. But the first step isn't adoption — it's understanding what you're agreeing to.

Have you read the terms of the AI tools you're using?
