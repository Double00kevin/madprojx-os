# madprojx — LinkedIn Daily Content Framework

## Posting Cadence

**Daily.** One post per day, 5-7 days per week. Consistency is the algorithm play and the audience-building play.

## Content Pillars Mapped to Days

| Day | Pillar | Post Type | Example Hook |
|-----|--------|-----------|-------------|
| Monday | AI Made Simple | Educational | "Most small businesses already have AI tools. They just don't know it." |
| Tuesday | AI Security for Small Business | Warning/Advisory | "I checked the data policies of 5 popular AI tools this week. Here's what I found." |
| Wednesday | Practical AI Use Cases | Case Study | "A 15-person HVAC company cut their scheduling time by 6 hours a week. Here's how." |
| Thursday | AI Mistakes to Avoid | Cautionary | "Three businesses called me this month after the same AI mistake." |
| Friday | Personal/Industry Take | Opinion/Hot Take | "Unpopular opinion: most small businesses should NOT be using ChatGPT for customer emails." |
| Saturday | Quick Tip / Checklist | Actionable | "Before you sign up for any AI tool, ask these 3 questions." |
| Sunday | Story / Reflection | Narrative | "Twenty years in cybersecurity taught me one thing that applies perfectly to AI adoption." |

This is a framework, not a rigid schedule. Swap days as needed. The point is covering all four pillars plus personal brand-building every week.

## Post Formats (Rotate These)

### The Hook + Lesson (most common)
- Bold opening line that stops the scroll
- 3-5 sentences of context or story
- The lesson or takeaway
- End with a question or invitation to comment

### The List Post
- "5 things every small business owner should check before using AI"
- Numbered, scannable, actionable
- Use sparingly — max 2 per week

### The Contrarian Take
- Challenge conventional wisdom about AI
- "Everyone says X. Here's why that's wrong for small businesses."
- Back it up with experience, not just opinion

### The Mini Case Study
- Anonymized client story or realistic scenario
- Problem → what they tried → what actually worked → result
- Specific numbers when possible (hours saved, cost, timeline)

### The Personal Story
- Something from Kevin's 20 years in cybersecurity that connects to AI today
- Humanizes the brand, builds trust
- Don't overuse — once a week max

### The Quick Tip
- Single, actionable piece of advice
- Under 100 words
- Great for weekends when engagement is lighter

## Writing Rules

1. **First line is everything.** If it doesn't stop the scroll, nothing else matters.
2. **One idea per post.** If you have two ideas, that's two posts.
3. **Write at a 7th grade reading level.** Not because the audience is dumb — because everyone scrolls fast.
4. **No jargon without definition.** If you say "LLM," you say "large language model (the technology behind tools like ChatGPT)" the first time.
5. **End with engagement.** Question, invitation to share their experience, or a strong closing statement.
6. **150-300 words.** Long enough to say something real, short enough to read in 60 seconds.
7. **Security angle in at least 2 of every 7 posts.** This is the differentiator. Keep it visible.
8. **No hashtag spam.** 3-5 relevant hashtags max. #AI #SmallBusiness #CyberSecurity #AIforBusiness

## Engagement Strategy (15-20 min/day)

- **Before posting:** Spend 10 minutes commenting on other people's posts in your space. Thoughtful comments, not "Great post!" Give value in comments.
- **After posting:** Respond to every comment on your post within the first 2 hours. The algorithm rewards early engagement.
- **Weekly:** Find 3-5 new accounts posting about AI + small business. Follow, comment, build relationships.
- **Never:** Auto-comment, use engagement pods, or post generic motivational content.

## Metrics to Track Weekly

| Metric | What It Tells You |
|--------|-------------------|
| Impressions per post | Reach — is the algorithm showing your content? |
| Engagement rate | Resonance — are people interacting? |
| Profile views | Curiosity — are people checking you out? |
| Connection requests | Trust — are people wanting to stay connected? |
| DMs / inbound inquiries | Revenue signal — are people reaching out for help? |

## Content Source Pipeline (Current — Manual)

1. Kevin captures ideas throughout the day (voice memos, notes, Obsidian ideas-bank)
2. Draft posts in Claude Desktop using brand voice from positioning.md
3. Review, edit, add personal voice
4. Publish directly on LinkedIn
5. Save final version to areas/content/drafts/ with date and pillar tag
